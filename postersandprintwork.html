Particle Physics Poster

This Poster was my attempt to showcase the standard model of particle physics in minimal modern style. First I decided to arrange the particles circularly. In the center rests the Higgs Boson, and each successive ring corresponds to sets of more complex fundamental particles.

Malaria: Film Poster

A film poster for an in house feature film. The heavy shadows and yellow tint lends itself to the neo noir feel of the piece. The figure holds a gun and a peanut butter and jelly sandwich. The juxtaposition between the two items mimics the film’s tendency to focus on lighter matters in the midst of terrific violence.

Julius Caesar: Poster

When beginning this design process I focused on the themes of Conspiracy and Brutality. In order to understand the visual context to express this information I met with the director. Since the play was being produced in a kabuki style, I decided on a minimal color palette of white, black, and red. From there I began working with the idea of expressing the hidden enemy. I ultimately decided to express this as a pair of eyes in the dark, the first glimpse of a predator in the deepest night.

The eyes were female for a subtle connection to the production’s cast. The eyes on this poster are Cassius’ eyes. Choosing them to be blue added a subtle chilling aspect as well as providing subtle contrast to overall color palette, the cold stern eyes in the midst of the red blood brutality.

From there I chose fonts with good visibility at a distance for the informational text, and made the title text a stylized roman font to remind the viewer of the period amidst the modern artistic direction.

Finally below the information text I included the famous quote “et tu brute?” to remind the viewer of the play in case they were unsure, and also as a pun: asking the viewer if they wish to partake.

Middletown Poster

This poster was a promotional print for a production of Will Eno’s Middletown. Since the play discusses metaphysical concepts in the context of ordinary circumstances, I decided to prominently display the night sky juxtapose the silhouette of a small town. This arrangement highlights the contrast between the beauty and awe of the natural universe with the small life that we as people experience in our daily lives.
